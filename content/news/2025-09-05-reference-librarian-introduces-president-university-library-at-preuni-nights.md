President University, September 6, 2025 — As part of the PreUni Nights program, the President University Library held an online introduction session via Zoom, led by the Reference Librarian. The event aimed to welcome and familiarize new students with the library’s facilities, services, and digital resources.

![Documentation Introducing Mendeley](https://library.president.ac.id/assets/img/news/pages-1/content-sep-6.png)

During the session, students were guided through the essentials of accessing the library’s collections, including the Online Public Access Catalog (OPAC), digital library platforms, and e-resources available to support their studies. The Reference Librarian also explained borrowing procedures, library policies, and how to make the most of library assistance for academic needs.

The session ran smoothly and provided new students with a comprehensive overview of the library’s role in supporting academic activities. By the end of the event, participants had gained a clear understanding of how to begin using the library effectively as part of their learning experience at President University.

This introduction was part of the university’s broader initiative to ensure that freshmen are well-prepared for their academic journey. Through PreUni Nights, students not only get to know their campus environment but also gain early exposure to the tools and services that will support their success throughout their studies.
