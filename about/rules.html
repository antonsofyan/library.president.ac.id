General Rules

  1. Respects to other users are highly appreciated, such as:
    • Keeping to a minimum volume of voices while talking or taking part in a group discussion
    • Sleeping, smoking, using cell phones, radio, joking, playing online games outside the library.
    • Proper use of the library furniture.
    • Proper attired at all times in the library.
  2. Keeping the library and the collection clean and in good condition by:
    • Eating and drinking outside the library.
    • Putting trash on the trash cans.
    • Proper handling of walls, furniture and library materials (no marking, ripping off, breaking) etc.
  3. Keeping the library in good order by:
    • Using the library mainly for academic and research purposes.
    • Putting the books on the trolley provided after reading or removing from the Shelves.
    • Leaving the furniture in its original place.
  4. Not leaving belongings overnight in the library. The library shall not take any responsibility for loss of personal property or books already issued out.
  5. Report to the library immediately if you find otherâ€™s belongings or if you lose your belongings.
  6. The library staff has the privilege to ask students to leave the library if they are behaving inappropriately or causing disturbance to other users.
  7. The library has the privilege to give any kind of discipline acts to any user who disregards the library rules and regulations.

Who is Library user?

The following people reserve the right to be the members of the library.
Members are entitled to use all library services.

  • All registered students of the University,
  • Active Faculty and administrative staffs in the University,
  • Known members of the community and those with a genuine need,
    have limited access to the library with a special permission from the head of the library.

** Others may read the library materials and use the reading room in the library.

Borrowing Book
Procedures

  1. A user must be a registered student or active faculty and administrative staff in the current term to be able to use the library services.
  2. All users must present their ID Card before borrowing any library materials.
  3. Library materials may not be removed from the library unless the library staff has properly issued them out.
  4. All borrowed materials must be returned on or before the due date.
  5. Students or staff are not allowed to borrow library materials on behalf of others or transfer borrowed materials to others.
  6. Students with overdue materials and overdue fines will not be allowed to borrow books.

How to borrow a locker key?

  1. Go to the circulation desk to borrow a locker key
    • Tell the library staff that you want to use the locker
    • Give your identity card in exchange for a locker key
    • You are not allowed to borrow the locker key on behalf of others or use other peopleâ€™s ID card to borrow the locker key
  2. Go to the Locker
    • Put your bag and other stuff in the library locker except for wallet, handphone, and laptop.
    • The locker can only be used while visiting the library, not overnight
  3. Upon Leaving Library
    • Take your bag and other stuff out of the locker
    • Return your locker key in exchange for your identity card
    • If you lose your locker key, report to the Circulation Desk and pay a penalty
  4. Overnight Locker Rules
    • The library staff will take out your stuff if left overnight. The library is not responsible for any losses.
