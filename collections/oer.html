OPEN EDUCATIONAL RESOURCES (OER)

Learning and teaching materials that can be shared, edited, and reused without obtaining permission from the owner are known as Open Educational Resources, or OERs. Since the producer of OER material has lifted certain copyright restrictions, it is readily accessible to the public and uses under an open license in the public domain. For academics looking for a free and authorized alternative information source to aid in their research and study, President University OER is available.

The following are the tenets of OER:

  • OER documents can be downloaded, printed, and saved using retain.
  • Reuse refers to the acceptance of content reuse.
  • Using revise, you can modify the material to fit your requirements.
  • Blending several OER materials together to create a new work is made feasible by remix.
  • Redistribute refers to the sharing of content in either its original or altered format.
